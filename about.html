Before We Start

Six questions I ask every potential client. Flip each card to understand my process and what to expect when working together.

01 / 06
What does success look like for this project?
Why I Ask
I need to understand your core objectives—whether that's conversion rates, user engagement, or brand perception. This shapes every technical decision I make, from animation complexity to performance budgets.
02 / 06
Who is your primary user, and what frustrates them?
Why I Ask
Empathy drives interface design. Knowing your users' pain points helps me prioritize features, simplify workflows, and create intuitive navigation patterns that actually solve problems.
03 / 06
What is your realistic timeline and budget?
Why I Ask
Transparency prevents scope creep. I use this to recommend the right approach—whether that's an MVP for quick validation or a polished product for market launch. Constraints breed creativity.
04 / 06
How will we measure progress and quality?
Why I Ask
I establish clear milestones and feedback loops early. This includes code review schedules, demo cadence, and performance benchmarks (Lighthouse scores, accessibility audits) to ensure we stay aligned.
05 / 06
What existing tech stack or constraints must I respect?
Why I Ask
Legacy systems, CMS requirements, or team expertise matter. I architect solutions that integrate smoothly with your infrastructure rather than forcing incompatible technologies that create technical debt.
06 / 06
What happens after launch?
Why I Ask
I build for maintainability. Understanding your long-term plans—whether that's scaling, adding features, or handoff to an internal team—influences documentation, component structure, and deployment strategy.
